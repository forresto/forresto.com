What may be Japan's first contra dance went very well this
Sunday.... I was nervous because it was the first time that I
have put together a program and called all of the dances. The
program works slowly to the contra dance concept, and the crowd was
able to get it. I should have concentrated on how to get back
into the dance from waiting out on the ends more when teaching, because
that's how all of the contras seemed to break down around 3.5 or 4
minutes into the dance. Maybe 3 minute musical tracks are the
answer. Anyhow, I was thrilled that it worked as well as it did,
especially since we were working with a language barrier and a
mostly-beginner crowd.

I was a little bit nervous about calling, as well, because I haven't in awhile. For most of the drive down to Hanamaki I played my contra mix and called the dances out loud. I bet that looked funny to other drivers. Once we started dancing it all worked out like riding a bicycle... I was calling and flat-footing like I was born to do it. It was fun! I only yelled at them once, and I think they forgave.... 待って待って待って！ (sheepish grin)

Presbyterians are always talking about looking for their "calling." I think I might have found my calling -- being, ahem, calling -- for at least my time in Japan.
