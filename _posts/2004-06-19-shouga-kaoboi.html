My parents left yesterday morning, and I want to write a big post about it, but I'm still processing... it was a bit of a whirlwind. So for now I give you the recipe for the new drink sweeping Japan, Asia, and the World:

生姜カオボイ
1/9 生姜シロップ *
5/9 豆乳
3/9 好きなウィスキー

* 生姜シロップ = 切った生姜 （黒）砂糖 水 を煮染めて

The Shouga Kaoboi
1/9 Ginger Syrup *
5/9 Unsweetened Soy Milk
3/9 Whiskey of choice

* Ginger Syrup = equal parts ginger slices, (brown) sugar, and water boiled down to 1/2 consistency... it gets syrupy. You can then bake the leftover ginger slices at 110 C for a couple hours to make ginger candy. Coat them in sugar first if you want them to turn out like crystallized ginger. I sliced mine super-thin, and they only took an hour to dry out into sweet/ginger-spicy chippy things. Yum. Also, the syrup can be mixed with soda water for ginger ale, easy-peasy.
